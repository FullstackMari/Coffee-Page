Best Practices for Home Espresso

  1. Preparation Basics:
    • Rinse Your Grouphead: Before pulling a shot, always rinse your grouphead by running an empty shot through the portafilter. This prevents heat loss to the portafilter and ensures a consistent extraction.
  2. Supplies You‚Äôll Need:
    • Digital Scale: Precise measurements matter. Use a digital scale to weigh your coffee grounds and monitor extraction times.
    • Milk Pitcher: For lattes and cappuccinos, a good milk pitcher is essential for steaming silky microfoam.
    • Double-Walled Espresso Cups: Keep your shots warm and showcase that crema!
    • Tamp Mat: Protect your countertop and tamp your coffee evenly.
    • Knockbox: Dispose of used coffee grounds conveniently.
  3. Pre-shot Rituals:
    • Heat Your Cup: Pour hot water into your cup before brewing. A warm cup helps maintain the ideal espresso temperature.
    • Steam Wand Hygiene: Always purge your steam wand before and after frothing milk to prevent milk residue buildup.
  4. Bean Storage Tips:
    • Airtight Container: Store your coffee beans in an airtight container away from light and moisture.
    • Grinder Dosage: Only grind what you‚Äôll use in a few days to keep your coffee fresh.
  5. Explore Specialty Coffee Shops:
    • Visit specialty coffee shops to taste different beans and learn about their origins. This knowledge will help you replicate flavors at home.
